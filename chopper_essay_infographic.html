The Nature of the Blade

This is the story of the Chopper‚Äînot merely a tool of wood and steel, but a metaphor for those among us whose very essence has become one of cutting, slicing, and breaking others.

Consider the axe in its simplicity. Day after day, it strikes against the grain of wood, fulfilling its purpose with mechanical precision. Yet with each blow, something within it changes. The sharp edge that once cleaved cleanly through timber grows dull, worn down by the very act that defines it.

Every chopper must eventually meet their match, every cutter will be cut to size.

The Cycle of Self-Destruction

The deterioration runs deeper than mere bluntness. Just as continuous chopping can crack the metal head of an axe or splinter its wooden handle, those who persist in hurting others often find themselves caught in a cycle of self-destruction. Their lives become fragments of what they once were‚Äîmiserable, unbearable, filled with the echoes of the pain they have caused.

How Choppers Are Born

How, then, are choppers born? The answer lies not in inherent evil but in the all-too-human tendency toward self-preservation and the worship of "me, myself, and I." Live and let live is the secret to harmony. Do not feed tigers so much that they eventually overpower even you.
